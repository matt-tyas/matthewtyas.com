University of Leeds Personal Tutoring Model: indicative agenda

Year 1, meeting 1 (within first two weeks of semester 1)

  • Discuss and confirm module choice/registration and advise as necessary;
  • If not already explained in induction week, explain PT system - how it supports students in normal course of academic personal and professional development; how referral role works to help support student through particular difficulties; how PT is student's first point-of-contact within Department/School
  • Explain that effective personal tutoring is a partnership between tutor and tutee where the tutee must also fulfil their responsibilities

2. Becoming a student at the University of Leeds
1. Have you registered?
More info

Every student must register at the start of each academic session in order to attend lectures, receive tuition, use University facilities, etc. When you register you will get a student ID card, giving you access to University facilities such as computing and the Library. You can get further information at leeds.ac.uk/studentguide or by emailing Student Administration.

2. Did you attend the introductory sessions and induction events?
More info

If you didn’t attend, contact your departmental administrator to get the information you missed.

3. Are you aware of Skills@Library and how it can help?
More info

Skills@Library has information, resources and online tutorials to help you develop some of the core skills for successful study. Topics include finding and evaluating academic information, reading more effectively, improving your writing skills, and referencing your work correctly to avoid plagiarism.

4. Are you aware of The Partnership?
More info

The Partnership was developed by students and staff and describes the expectations of all members of the University community. We recommend reading the website then perhaps discussing it with your personal tutor.

5. Are you aware of the Taught Student Handbook?
More info

The Taught Student Handbook brings together all the information you need on University rules and regulations, examinations and assessment. It also gives sources of academic and pastoral support across campus.

6. Are you aware of the rules on plagiarism, cheating and malpractice?
More info

The rules are in the Taught Student Handbook – it is very important that you understand them. For specific advice about plagiarism, see www.ldu.leeds.ac.uk/plagiarism or http://library.leeds.ac.uk/skills-plagiarism.
